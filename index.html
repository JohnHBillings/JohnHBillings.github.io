I am an economics PhD student at the University of East Anglia (anticipated graduation 2026) and the volunteer Research Committee Chair at Pro-Housing Pittsburgh. My research is in Behavioral and Experimental Economics, with a focus on how people make decisions and how we can rigorously test decision-making models in controlled settings.

A central theme of my work is social preferences—how individuals make choices that affect not only themselves, but others. I apply these tools to questions in Urban Economics and Public Policy, where my interests center on how zoning and tax policy shape housing production.

At the intersection of these areas is Behavioral Political Economy: understanding how policymakers, stakeholders, and voters respond to incentives and framing, and how these insights can be used to design zoning and tax policies that expand housing supply and improve affordability.
